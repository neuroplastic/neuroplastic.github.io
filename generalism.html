Pursuit of Generalism:

I have pursued expert generalism (e.g. systematically reading and taking notes across all knowledge) since ca. 1986.

My solution for the many difficulties in academic careers with such an orientation, has been not to attempt one.

Since ca. 1996, my goal has been to publish entirely in the semantic web. (Actually, this goal formed ca. 1975, when i became obsessed with the Macropaedia of the Encyclopedia Britannica.)

Restated: since publishing to humans is (increasingly) problematic, my goal has been to publish in a sustainably machine-readable form, through which the full graph of novel contributions can be actively discovered and re-used, decoupled from the limits to human attention and memory.

Yes, i have been writing to a future AGI.

Importantly, i have never believed that text mining, NLP, and ML will be able to read and interpret human meanings correctly. Since reading Hesse’s Magister Ludi: The Glass Bead Game ca. 1988, i have always seen it as necessary that humans directly represent their meanings in formal logic.

The semantic web field began advancing rapidly towards such a solution ca. 2015. As of 2021, i became confident that i can convert my knowledge graph into hybrid human- and machine-readable form, without unnecessary effort, and without having to redo everything (again) down the line.

I am currently doing this conversion, and will be publishing to multiple fields soon. I will begin posting PDFs + RDF alter in 2022.
