An independent researcher and software developer, integrating cognitive science, sociobiology, hyperweb development, and political economy.

Research goals

long-term

  • to develop the internet and web as a system that translates the adaptational mechanisms of evolutionary biology to planetary scale
  • to develop the internet and web as a system that enables continual integrated adaptations across local-global scales
  • to model humanity not in the terms of selfish genes, but as an evolutionary mechanism designed to both trigger and manage the transition of the planetary biome to self-managing homeostasis, through first causing and then adaptating to climate change

mid-term

  • developing the open web as a critical component enabling emergent adaptation to climate change
  • providing humans with the legal status and functional capabilities of autonomous agents in a formally open web

near-term

  • accelerating the widespread adoption of the open web platform over commercial silos for personal and organisational data

Current projects:

  1. P2P Science:
    • a set of four applications for:
      • decentralising scientific communications at the server
      • re-integrating scientific communications at the client
      • thin-slicing scientific communications on mobile
  2. Finnish Social Mesh:
    • nationalised platform cooperativism through combining personal datastores with meshnets
  3. Peergen:
    • a system of personal datastores that makes the individual the primary researcher over their own healthdata
    • in biomedical terms, Peergen is a system for N-of–1 personal -omics at scale, in which feedback loops between data, interpretation, and patient are not avoided, but embraced
  4. Pod Zero:
    • a personal data vault architecture interpreting users as autonomous agents in a formally open web
  5. Umwelt:
    • a ladder of scientific communications, including VR and AR, for highly integrated remote-working research cultures
